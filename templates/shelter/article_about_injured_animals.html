What to Do When You Find an Injured Animal

Your Guide to Helping Animals in Need

1. Assess the Situation

Before approaching the injured animal, take a moment to assess the situation:

  • Check for traffic or other dangers that may pose a risk to you or the animal.
  • Determine if the animal is truly injured or just scared.
  • Look for signs of distress, such as unusual vocalizations or inability to move.

2. Approach with Caution

When you feel it’s safe to approach, follow these steps:

  • Move slowly and calmly to avoid startling the animal.
  • Speak softly to reassure it; avoid sudden movements.
  • Use a towel or blanket to cover the animal if it allows, which can help calm it down.

3. Contact a Professional

It’s essential to get the animal to a professional as soon as possible:

  • Call a local veterinarian or animal rescue organization for guidance.
  • If necessary, transport the animal to the nearest veterinary clinic.
  • Provide the professionals with as much information as possible about the animal's condition.

4. Provide Basic First Aid

If you are trained in first aid and it’s safe to do so, you can provide basic care:

  • Control any bleeding by applying pressure with a clean cloth.
  • Keep the animal warm and comfortable until help arrives.
  • Avoid giving the animal food or water unless instructed by a veterinarian.

5. Prevent Future Incidents

After helping the injured animal, consider how to prevent similar situations:

  • Educate others about the importance of keeping pets safe and contained.
  • Advocate for animal welfare in your community to improve conditions for wildlife and pets.
  • Consider volunteering or donating to local animal shelters or rescue organizations.
